Natalie Rusk

Research Scientist

MIT Media Lab

About

Natalie Rusk is a Research Scientist at the MIT Media Lab, where she works on developing creative computing technologies and educational approaches. She has been instrumental in the development and implementation of Scratch worldwide.

Background

Dr. Rusk has extensive experience in educational technology and computer science education. She holds a PhD in Computer Science and has dedicated her career to making programming accessible and engaging for young learners.

Key Contributions

  • Co-developer of Scratch programming environment
  • Designer of ScratchJr for younger children
  • Leader in creative computing curriculum development
  • Advocate for equity in computer science education

Research Focus

  • Creative computing in K-12 education
  • Designing tools for young programmers
  • Professional development for educators
  • Equity and inclusion in computing education

Global Impact

Dr. Rusk has worked with educators around the world to implement Scratch in diverse educational contexts. She has helped train thousands of teachers and has contributed to making creative computing accessible to underserved communities.
