Mitchel Resnick

Creator & Lead Developer of Scratch

MIT Media Lab

About

Mitchel Resnick is the LEGO Papert Professor of Learning Research at the MIT Media Lab, where he leads the Lifelong Kindergarten group. He developed the ideas underlying the Scratch programming language and online community, used by millions of young people around the world.

Background

Dr. Resnick earned his PhD in Computer Science from MIT and has been a pioneer in the field of creative computing education for over two decades. His work focuses on how new technologies can engage people in creative learning experiences.

Key Contributions

  • Creator of Scratch programming language
  • Developer of programmable LEGO bricks
  • Advocate for creative computing in education
  • Author of "Lifelong Kindergarten" book

Awards & Recognition

  • Winner of the 2011 McGraw Prize in Education
  • Recipient of the 2018 AECT Outstanding Book Award
  • Named one of the "Top 10 Minds in Educational Technology"

Publications

Dr. Resnick has published numerous papers on creative computing, constructionist learning, and educational technology. His book "Lifelong Kindergarten: Cultivating Creativity through Projects, Passion, Peers, and Play" outlines his philosophy of learning.
