Karen Brennan

Education Researcher

Harvard Graduate School of Education

About

Karen Brennan is an Associate Professor at the Harvard Graduate School of Education, where she studies creative computing and computational thinking in education. Her research focuses on how young people learn to program and how programming can support learning across disciplines.

Background

Dr. Brennan earned her PhD from the MIT Media Lab and has been a leading researcher in computational thinking and creative computing education. She has worked extensively with Scratch and has developed frameworks for assessing computational thinking.

Key Contributions

  • Pioneer in computational thinking assessment
  • Developer of Creative Computing curriculum
  • Researcher in programming education
  • Advocate for inclusive computing education

Research Areas

  • Computational thinking in K-12 education
  • Creative computing pedagogy
  • Assessment of programming skills
  • Design-based learning approaches

Notable Work

Dr. Brennan developed the Creative Computing Curriculum Guide, which has been used by educators worldwide to teach programming with Scratch. She has also created assessment tools and frameworks that help educators understand and evaluate computational thinking development.

Publications & Impact

Her research has been published in leading educational technology journals, and she regularly speaks at conferences about the intersection of programming, creativity, and learning.
