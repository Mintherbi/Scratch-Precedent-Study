What is Creative Computing?

Creative computing is an approach to computing education that emphasizes creativity, personal expression, and meaningful engagement with technology. Scratch is an ideal platform for creative computing because it allows students to create interactive stories, games, and animations.

Key Principles

  • Create: Students build projects that are personally meaningful
  • Collaborate: Work together and share ideas
  • Connect: Relate projects to personal interests and experiences
  • Question: Encourage critical thinking about technology

Project Ideas

Here are some creative computing projects students can create with Scratch:

  • Interactive storytelling with multiple characters
  • Educational games that teach specific concepts
  • Digital art projects with moving elements
  • Music and sound compositions
  • Simulations of real-world phenomena

Computational Thinking Skills

Through creative computing with Scratch, students develop:

  • Decomposition: Breaking down complex problems
  • Pattern Recognition: Finding similarities in different parts of a problem
  • Abstraction: Focusing on essential features while ignoring irrelevant details
  • Algorithms: Creating step-by-step solutions

Assessment in Creative Computing

Rather than traditional tests, creative computing assessment focuses on:

  • Project portfolios showing growth over time
  • Reflection journals documenting the design process
  • Peer feedback and collaboration skills
  • Demonstration of computational thinking concepts
